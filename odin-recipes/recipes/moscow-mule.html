Moscow Mule Recipe

Description

The Moscow Mule is a classic cocktail with a fascinating origin. It was reportedly created in the United States during the 1940s, a time when vodka was relatively unknown in the country. The cocktail's investion is often attributed to a collection between John G. Martin, Jack Morgan, and Sophie Berezinski. Martin, a liquor distributor, was struggling to sell his surplus of ginger beer, and Morgan, owner of the Cock 'n' Bull pub in Hollywood, had an abundance of vodka. They combined these two ingredients with a dash of lime juice, and the Moscow Mule was born.

This iconic drink is typically served in a copper mug, which not only keeps the cocktail ice-cold but also adds to its distinctive charm. The Moscow Mule's is known for its refreshing and slightly spicy flavor profile, making it a popular choice for those seeking a crisp and zesty cocltail option. Over the years, the Moscow Mule has become a beloved classic, enjoyed by cocktail enthusiasts around the world.

Ingredients

  • 1.5oz Vokda
  • 0.5oz Lime Juice
  • 6oz Ginger Beer
  • 1 Lime Wedge

Steps

  1. Pour vodka and lime juice into copper mug.
  2. Add ample ice cubes. Top off with Ginger Beer.
  3. Stir to combine. Garnish with lime wedge.
