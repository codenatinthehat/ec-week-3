Old Fashioned Recipe

Description

The Old Fashioned cocktail is a timeless classic with a history dating back to the early 19th century. It's considered one of the oldest known cocktails, and its origins can be traced to the early days of American cocktail culture. Originally known simply as a "whiskey cocktail", it consisted of a straightforward combination of whiskey, sugar, water, and bitters. As time passed, the cocktail evolved, and the name "Old Fashioned" emerged to distinguish it from newer, more elaborate concoctions.

What sets the Old Fashioned apart is its simplicity and focus on the pure, unadulterated flavors of the whiskey. The result of the drink is a beautifully balanced cocktail that allows the character of the whiskey to shine through.

Ingredients

  • 2oz Whiskey
  • Bitters
  • 1 teaspoon Water
  • Orange peel

Steps

  1. Add the water and 3 dashes of bitters into a short whiskey glass.
  2. Stir to combine. Add a large ice ball to the glass and pour whiskey on top.
  3. Garnish with orange peel.
