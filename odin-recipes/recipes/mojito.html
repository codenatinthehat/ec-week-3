Mojito Recipe

Description

The mojito, tracing its roots to Cuba, emerged from a historical blend of ingredients. Incorporating rum, mint, lime, and sugar, it achieved international acclaim, notably through Hemingway's affection. Its versatile taste complements various cuisines, including seafood, tropical fruits, ad spicy fare, offering a flavorful connection to Caribbean traditions.

The mojito is a refreshing and zesty beverage, the perfect drink to enjoy on a hot summer day. Whether enjoyed in the bustling streets of Havanna or at a beachside bar, the mojito remains a timeless classic that embodies the spirit of Cuba's rich history and hospitality.

Ingredients

  • 2 Ounces of Rum
  • 1 Lime
  • 6 Sprig of Mint
  • 6oz Club Soda
  • Pinch of Sugar

Steps

  1. In a sturdy, tall cocktail glass, add the sugar and 4 sprigs of mint.
  2. Muddle the mint with the sugar, until mint is fragrant.
  3. Add rum and lime juice. Stir to dissolve sugar. Fill the glass with ice.
  4. Top off glass with club soda.
  5. To garnish, place a lime round on glass and tuck remaining mint sprigs into glass.
